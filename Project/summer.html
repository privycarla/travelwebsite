Let’s Talk About Batanes: A Traveller’s Dream


“The only trip you will ever regret is the one you don’t take.”

Batanes. My dream destination. A destination which remained elusive until recently. I have been procrastinating for so long. It was a case of should I go now, or wait till next month? Will the weather be good? What if the weather gets bad when I am there and I get stranded? This was one trip with a lot ifs and buts. But then finally, I threw all cautions up in there air and opted to follow my gut feeling.

Batanes is the northernmost part of the Philippines. The smallest province, a part of which is nearer to Taiwan than Luzon. I am pretty sure that most if not all Filipino travelers include Batanes in their bucket list.

My first memories of Batanes came from textbooks during grade school. Pictures of stone houses, the Ivatan people with their local head gear, and the rolling hills with wild horses grazing on never ending green pasture lands, really appealed to me. But going there is not easy and can be expensive. The unpredictable weather and prohibitive costs of plane fare are the reasons why travellers and tourists seldom visit the so-called paradise of the north.

As the Bombardier Q400 of Philippine Airlines left Manila and flew over mainland Luzon, I was amazed with the view from my window seat. I have never gone this far north of Manila. Unlike the scattered islands in the Visayas, the view consisted of large chunks of islands where you can see white outlines around it caused by the crashing waves. The trip from Manila to Basco lasted for an hour and a half. While the plane was manuevering, I can already see the landscapes of Batanes. A lighthouse stood by a cliff. The rolling hills were greeting me from the horizon. It was indeed breath-taking. I was saying to myself – Wow! I am finally here! F I N A L L Y !

Upon arrival, you will need to register at the tourism office and pay a fee of P300. I booked in advance, a fan room with Marfel’s Lodge for 4 nights amounting to P2,000. I also booked with BISUMI Travel and Tours for P4,999, which consisted of the Batan North Tour, Batan South Tour and Sabtang Tour. Both representatives from Marfel and Bisumi were at the airport to meet me and brought me to the lodge to rest up to 12 noon. After which the tour began.

Some Reasons To Make Boracay Island Your Next Vacation Destination


“ Boracay is the kind of island you will never want to leave. I’ve traveled to more than 50 countries and there are not many places where I’ve returned 5 times. Boracay is one of them ”
- Sabrina Lovina (travel blogger)

Welcome to paradise. Boracay is a tiny tropical island where people from all over the world come to enjoy beach life, watersport activities, and a vibrant nightlife. It’s located in the mid-west of the Philippines – a one hour flight away from the country’s capital (Manila). Boracay’s attraction isn’t limited to just a few things. It’s the combination of many things that makes this island unique.

So without further talk, take a look over my shoulder as I show you Boracay.

1. Its Stunning White Beach

White Beach is the main beach of Boracay. It’s 4 kilometers long and has resorts, restaurants and nightlife venues along it.

The main reasons why people love this beach are:
- Beautiful shallow and still turquoise water – great for swimming
- Amazing views while you eat & drink right in front of the beach
- Powdery white sand that never gets hot br - A beautiful sandy beach path, with shade

2. Eight More Beautiful Beaches


Boracay Island has not 1, 2 or 3 beautiful beaches but 9, and all within close proximity of each other since the
island is very small (7 by 1.5 kilometers).

Each beach also has its own attractiveness. Some are amazingly beautiful, some are good for snorkeling, some have privacy, and some have a mix of those and other things.

3. The Friendly Local People


When you arrive in Boracay one of the first things you’ll notice is the friendliness and openness of the local
people. That’s not where it stops though…

Filipinos also speak English as their second language, so there is no language barrier (unlike as in so many other
South East Asian countries). Because of this you can easily connect and make friends with locals as well as with
other tourists.

4. The Vibrant Nightlife

Meeting new people or just having a fun night out with your friends is easy with many nightlife venues to choose
from. Plus alcohol is cheap so if you like to drink you’re in the right place.

Discover the Breathtaking Beauty of Palawan


Palawan is a world-renowned destination for its breathtaking land and seascapes, offering unparalleled cultural, adventure, and nature experiences. Visitors can immerse themselves in trekking through hills, valleys, and rivers, as well as snorkeling and diving in pristine waters. Additionally, there are diverse festivals held throughout the year, allowing visitors to partake and celebrate with the locals. Come and discover the truly remarkable experiences that await you in Palawan!


Palawan facts:

Nickname: Philippines’ Last Frontier

Capital: Puerto Princesa

Palawan has 4 airports: Coron Busuanga, El Nido, Puerto Princesa, and San Vicente

Most popular destinations: El Nido and Coron


Discover the heart and soul of the Philippines on the pristine white beaches and dramatic rock formations of Palawan. This island province is an adventurer’s dream, bursting with exotic flora and fauna, primeval caves, and secluded beaches. From exploring indigenous settlements to kayaking among limestone cliffs, Palawan offers endless opportunities for adventure.

Saint Paul Subterranean River National Park is not a dive site: Palawan province boasts some of the world’s most challenging dive sites, with marine reserves, coral reefs, and subterranean rivers, including the UNESCO World Heritage sites of Tubbataha Reef.

Additionally, Palawan is home to another UNESCO World Heritage site – the Saint Paul Subterranean River National Park. This underground river system is surrounded by well- preserved limestone formations and a forest teeming with wildlife, making it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

But Palawan isn’t just for adrenaline junkies – it’s also a nature lover’s paradise, wrapped in a mantel of rainforests, majestic mountains, and beaches. Named “World’s Best Island” by several Travel Magazines, Palawan is an untamed land that lives up to its image as the Last Frontier.

Baguio City: A Dream Place to Live


Imagine yourself standing in front of a window. As you closed your eyes the wind started to caress your face with its gentle touch and with its cool breeze refreshing your soul. The humming birds replace the loud noise in your mind, calming all your senses, making you feel relaxed in the world full of stress and pressure. Then slowly, open your eyes look at those beautiful flowers in the field and then shift your gaze to those green robust trees around. If you don’t see what I described and longed to experience all I mentioned above, then Baguio City Philippines is the best place for you to live in.

Baguio City is the best place to live at, and there are a lot of facts to prove this. First, there are a lot of beautiful places in Baguio City. It is undeniable that Baguio has a colourful glow that no other cities can equal. The rich culture of the City has a great contribution to its glow and that is the reason why a lot of artists considered to make Baguio as their home.

Second, creativity overflows all over the place. If you love music, arts, literature, and other things that are fuelled by creativity, then Baguio City is the best place for you. The serenity of the place can stimulate the mind to think deeper and it encourages the mind to look wider with a greater and clearer perspective to the world. The breath-taking beauty of nature will capture you and swallow you which will leave you stunned with the encounter of the greatness of the Almighty. The quietness of the place will surely sanctify your ears and with the soft whisper of the wind you will be able to hear yourself more than the screaming nonsense of the world.

Another reason to choose Baguio City is that of its market place. Shopping is one of the most awesome things you can do in this city. There are lots of quality products to choose from and it comes from different varieties. You can also find interestingly unique local products available in the market and tasty delicacies that will surely satisfy your eyes and your craving and won’t leave your budget hanging.

The city itself is located and surrounded by mountains, but it is a well-developed city with a lot of commercial establishments in it. You won’t be bored because there’s a lot of places you can hop at and hop again with your friends and families. It is also worth considering that, the city has the best academic institutions that provide high standard education for your family. And if you’re still not convinced that Baguio is not the place for you, well you should be knowing this. Every year the property value in Baguio City goes up and that is another reason for you to consider owning a property there.

With everything I have mentioned above about Baguio City, I can say that it is really a great place. And it’s not only because of its temperature but there’s more than that. Baguio is great because it embraces everyone with its hospitality whether you are a local, a tourist, or the most diverse person in the world. The citizens will always greet you with those warm welcoming smiles and I believe that it is the greatest reason for you to consider Baguio as a place to call Home.
